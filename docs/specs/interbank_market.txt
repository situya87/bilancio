SPECIFICATION: CALL-AUCTION INTERBANK RESERVE MARKET
=====================================================

Version: 1.0
Status:  Draft
Date:    2026-03-03


1. OVERVIEW
-----------

Banks trade overnight reserve loans via a daily call auction that runs
after client payment settlement (Phase C). The central bank corridor
sets interest rate bounds. Surplus banks lend reserves to deficit banks
at a single market-clearing rate r*. Any residual deficit after the
auction is covered by the CB standing facility.

This replaces the current bilateral matching mechanism in
src/bilancio/engines/interbank.py with a proper call-auction design.


2. DESIGN DECISIONS
--------------------

Post-settlement only.
  The auction runs after client payment nets are settled. Banks know
  their actual reserve position before submitting orders. There is no
  pre-positioning or speculative reserve demand.

Banks on one side only.
  A surplus bank can only lend (submit an ask). A deficit bank can
  only borrow (submit a bid). No bank appears on both sides. Position
  determines role; there is no strategic choice of side.

Single clearing rate.
  The auction produces one clearing rate r* at which all matched
  trades execute. This is a standard call-auction mechanism: no
  bilateral rate negotiation.

Surplus lends to deficit.
  Reserve transfers flow from surplus banks to deficit banks. The
  direction is mechanically determined by the reserve position.

CB is true backstop.
  The interbank auction runs first. Only unfilled borrowers access
  the CB lending facility. This creates a clear pecking order:
  interbank market first, central bank for residual.

Overnight maturity.
  All interbank loans mature the next day (day+1). This is the
  simplest possible tenor and matches the daily cycle.

Risk-free.
  Interbank loans carry no counterparty credit risk assessment.
  Banks do not evaluate each other's creditworthiness. The reserve
  system guarantees settlement. This is a deliberate simplification
  for the initial implementation.

No new parameters.
  The limit rate formula uses only existing BankProfile fields:
  corridor midpoint M (from corridor_mid), corridor width Omega
  (from corridor_width), and reserve target R*. No additional
  tuning knobs are introduced.

Simple quantities.
  Each bank submits its full surplus or full deficit as the order
  quantity. There is no partial submission or reserve withholding.

Overnight repayments netted with client flows.
  Maturing interbank loan repayments are folded into the bilateral
  netting computation rather than settled as separate transfers.
  This reduces gross reserve flows and is operationally cleaner.


3. RESERVE POSITION COMPUTATION
---------------------------------

After client payment settlement, each bank i has:

  R_i   = current reserves (post-settlement)
  N_i   = net obligation from combined bilateral nets, including both
          client payment obligations and interbank loan repayments
  R*_i  = reserve target (from BankProfile.reserve_target_ratio times
          total deposits)

The reserve position is:

  x_i = R_i - N_i - R*_i

Interpretation:

  x_i > 0   surplus bank, potential lender
  x_i < 0   deficit bank, potential borrower
  x_i = 0   at target, excluded from auction


4. LIMIT RATE FORMULA
----------------------

Each bank's limit rate is derived mechanically from its reserve
position. There is no strategic rate choice.

Step 1: Normalize the position relative to target.

  normalized_i = clamp(x_i / R*_i, -1, +1)

  The clamp bounds the ratio to [-1, +1]. A bank whose surplus
  equals its entire target has normalized = +1. A bank whose
  deficit equals its entire target has normalized = -1.

Step 2: Compute the limit rate.

  limit_rate_i = M - (Omega / 2) * normalized_i

  where:
    M     = corridor midpoint  = BankProfile.corridor_mid(kappa)
    Omega = corridor width     = BankProfile.corridor_width(kappa)

Properties:

  At target (x = 0, normalized = 0):
    limit_rate = M
    The bank is indifferent at the corridor midpoint.

  Maximum surplus (normalized = +1):
    limit_rate = M - Omega/2 = r_floor
    The most liquid bank is willing to lend at the cheapest rate,
    which equals the CB deposit facility rate (corridor floor).

  Maximum deficit (normalized = -1):
    limit_rate = M + Omega/2 = r_ceiling
    The most stressed bank is willing to borrow at the most
    expensive rate, which equals the CB lending facility rate
    (corridor ceiling).

  Surplus banks (normalized > 0):
    limit_rate < M
    Willing to lend below the midpoint. Larger surplus means
    lower limit rate (more eager to lend).

  Deficit banks (normalized < 0):
    limit_rate > M
    Willing to borrow above the midpoint. Larger deficit means
    higher limit rate (more desperate to borrow).

This ensures every limit rate falls within [r_floor, r_ceiling],
the CB corridor bounds.


5. ORDER BOOK CONSTRUCTION
----------------------------

Surplus banks become sellers of reserves. Their limit rates are
ask prices. The sell side is sorted ascending by limit_rate (cheapest
ask first).

  Ask book: [(bank_id, quantity=x_i, limit_rate_i)]
            sorted by limit_rate ascending

Deficit banks become buyers of reserves. Their limit rates are bid
prices. The buy side is sorted descending by limit_rate (highest bid
first).

  Bid book: [(bank_id, quantity=|x_i|, limit_rate_i)]
            sorted by limit_rate descending

Banks with x_i = 0 are excluded from both sides.


6. AUCTION CLEARING
--------------------

The auction uses a standard call-auction crossing algorithm.

Step 1: Walk the book.
  Start from the cheapest ask and the highest bid. While the
  marginal ask rate is less than or equal to the marginal bid rate,
  match them.

Step 2: Determine volume at each match.
  volume = min(remaining_ask_quantity, remaining_bid_quantity)

  If the ask is fully filled, advance to the next ask. If the bid
  is fully filled, advance to the next bid. If both are exactly
  filled, advance both.

Step 3: Determine clearing rate.
  r* = (marginal_ask_rate + marginal_bid_rate) / 2

  The marginal ask and marginal bid are the last pair that crossed
  (ask <= bid). All matched trades execute at this single rate r*.

Step 4: Handle unfilled borrowers.
  Any bid quantity remaining after the book is exhausted represents
  a deficit that the interbank market could not fill. These borrowers
  proceed to the CB backstop facility.

Edge cases:

  No crossing.
    If the cheapest ask exceeds the highest bid, no trades occur.
    All borrowers go directly to the CB backstop.

  Single match.
    r* = midpoint of the single ask-bid pair.

  Equal rates.
    If ask == bid, they match. r* = that rate.


7. TRADE EXECUTION
-------------------

For each matched trade between lender L and borrower B:

  a) Transfer reserves from L to B.
     Call system.transfer_reserves(L, B, volume).
     This updates both banks' reserve balances atomically.

  b) Create an InterbankLoan record.
     Fields:
       lender_bank   = L
       borrower_bank = B
       amount        = volume
       rate          = r*
       issuance_day  = current_day
       maturity_day  = current_day + 1

     The loan is appended to banking.interbank_loans.

  c) Emit an InterbankAuctionTrade event.
     Fields:
       kind          = "InterbankAuctionTrade"
       day           = current_day
       lender        = L
       borrower      = B
       amount        = volume
       rate          = str(r*)
       clearing_rate = str(r*)
       maturity_day  = current_day + 1

If transfer_reserves raises a ValidationError (e.g., lender has
insufficient reserves due to a race condition), the trade is skipped
and logged as a warning.


8. OVERNIGHT REPAYMENT (NEXT DAY)
-----------------------------------

Interbank loans mature the day after issuance. Repayment is handled
by folding the obligation into the bilateral netting system rather
than settling it as a standalone transfer.

Identification.
  At the start of Phase C on day d, scan banking.interbank_loans for
  loans where maturity_day == d. These are the maturing loans.

Repayment amount.
  For each maturing loan:
    repayment = loan.amount + interest
    interest  = round(loan.amount * loan.rate)

  The interest calculation uses the loan's stored rate (r* from the
  auction that created it).

Netting integration.
  Each maturing loan generates a bilateral obligation:
    borrower_bank owes lender_bank the repayment amount.

  This obligation is added to the same bilateral netting matrix as
  client payment flows. The combined net determines the actual
  reserve transfer direction and magnitude between each bank pair.

  This means: if bank A owes bank B 1000 from client payments but
  bank B owes bank A 800 from a maturing interbank loan, the net
  transfer is only 200 from A to B.

Cleanup.
  After settlement completes, matured loans are removed from
  banking.interbank_loans.


9. PHASE C PIPELINE
--------------------

The full Phase C pipeline with call-auction interbank lending:

Step 1: compute_interbank_obligations()
  Scan banking.interbank_loans for loans maturing today.
  Return a list of (borrower, lender, repayment_amount) tuples.

Step 2: compute_combined_nets()
  Start with client payment bilateral nets (from ClientPayment
  events for today). Add interbank repayment obligations from
  Step 1. Result: combined bilateral net matrix.

Step 3: compute_bank_net_obligations()
  Aggregate the combined bilateral nets to per-bank positions.
  Each bank gets a single number: total net reserves owed (negative)
  or receivable (positive) from all counterparties combined.

Step 4: run_interbank_auction()
  a) Compute reserve positions x_i using post-settlement reserves,
     combined net obligations from Step 3, and reserve targets.
  b) Compute limit rates from positions.
  c) Build ask book (surplus banks) and bid book (deficit banks).
  d) Run call-auction crossing to find r* and matched trades.
  e) Execute trades (transfer reserves, create loans, emit events).
  f) Return list of trade events and the clearing rate.

Step 5: settle_nets_with_funding()
  Settle the combined bilateral nets from Step 2. Banks that
  received interbank funding in Step 4 now have the reserves to
  meet their obligations. Banks still short go to the CB backstop.

Step 6: finalize_interbank_repayments()
  Remove all matured loans (maturity_day == today) from
  banking.interbank_loans. These have been settled via the combined
  netting in Step 5.

Step 7: _run_cb_backstop()
  Any bank still below its reserve target after Steps 4-6 borrows
  from the CB standing facility at r_ceiling.


10. RESERVE PROJECTION INTEGRATION
-------------------------------------

Banks maintain a 10-day forward projection of reserve flows for
pricing purposes (the refresh_quote pipeline). Interbank loans must
be included in this projection.

For a lender bank that made a loan on day d:
  Day d:    outflow of loan.amount (reserves lent out)
  Day d+1:  inflow of loan.repayment_amount (principal + interest)

For a borrower bank that received a loan on day d:
  Day d:    inflow of loan.amount (reserves received)
  Day d+1:  outflow of loan.repayment_amount (principal + interest)

These flows are added to the existing projection alongside client
payment flows, CB loan repayments, and deposit interest.


11. PROPERTIES AND INVARIANTS
-------------------------------

Reserve conservation.
  The interbank auction does not create or destroy reserves. For
  every reserve unit transferred from a lender to a borrower, the
  lender's reserves decrease and the borrower's reserves increase
  by the same amount. The sum of all bank reserves is unchanged by
  interbank trading. Only the CB standing facility creates new
  reserves.

Clearing rate within corridor.
  r_floor <= r* <= r_ceiling. This follows from the limit rate
  formula: all limit rates are in [r_floor, r_ceiling] by
  construction, and r* is the midpoint of two such rates.

No negative reserves.
  system.transfer_reserves raises ValidationError if the source
  bank would go below zero reserves. Trades that would violate
  this constraint are skipped.

Overnight maturity.
  Every InterbankLoan has maturity_day = issuance_day + 1. There
  are no multi-day interbank loans in this specification.

One-sided participation.
  A bank with x > 0 appears only in the ask book. A bank with
  x < 0 appears only in the bid book. No bank is on both sides.

Deterministic clearing.
  Given the same reserve positions and the same BankProfile
  parameters, the auction produces the same r* and the same set
  of matched trades. There is no randomness in the clearing
  mechanism.

Monotonic limit rates.
  Surplus banks with larger surpluses have lower limit rates (more
  willing to lend cheaply). Deficit banks with larger deficits have
  higher limit rates (more willing to borrow expensively). This
  follows from the linear relationship between normalized position
  and limit rate.
